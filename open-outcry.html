02 — The Story Inside

What the internet can't find about Open Outcry.

The name. "Open outcry" is the trading method pit traders used on the Chicago Board of Trade floor — shouting, hand signals, organized chaos. Before electronic trading killed it in 2015, every trade in Chicago happened like this. The brewery is named after a dead art form of American financial history.

The owner. Jim Beck worked that floor. He's from Beverly — St. Barnabas Parish, grew up on these blocks. He didn't just buy a brewery with an interesting name. He bought a brewery named after the thing he used to do, in the neighborhood where he grew up. Katie Beck runs the day-to-day — she's been creating food content since 2014 with her blog "Kate the Baker." She already knows how to make things look good. The format is the only thing missing.

The beers. Every single one is named after a financial term. Black Scholes — the options pricing model. Dark Pool — off-exchange trading. Bang the Close — dumping orders at 3:59 PM. Speculator. Dodd-Frank. The Accountant. Big Board. Open Interest. Each beer is a story. Nobody is telling them.

The brewmaster. Wil Turner — 25 years of experience. Head pub brewer at Revolution Brewing for 7 years. Goose Island Beer Co. before that. He was a customer at Open Outcry before he was the brewmaster. He chose this place. Almost nobody outside Beverly knows he's here.

The rooftop. Beer garden with deck chairs, couches, live music building. Igloos in the winter. The best visual asset in the building — essentially zero documentation online.

The Point

The most interesting origin story in Chicago craft beer — a trading floor that became a brewery, beer names that read like a financial textbook, an owner who lived the story — doesn't exist anywhere online. That's the gap.

The Rule

Floor → Craft → Pour. Every video opens with something from the trading world — the name, the concept, the bit. The middle is the craft — how the beer is made, what goes into it. The resolve is always the pour. Same structure. Infinite variations.

Format 01 — The Market Report

Jim at the bar. 30 seconds. Weekly.

"Markets closed down 2% today. Black Scholes is on nitro. Dark Pool just came back from the barrel. The Speculator is moving." Quick cuts. Beer pours as the ticker. Jim plays the anchor — because he already was one.

Why it works here: Jim worked the actual floor. He's not cosplaying a trader — he was one. The authority is real. Cadence: 1x/week.

Format 02 — "What's a [Beer Name]?"

Jim holds the pint. Explains the term. Connects it to the beer.

"This is called Black Scholes. In 1973, Fischer Black and Myron Scholes figured out how to price risk mathematically. You know what you can't price? How good this stout is." Sip.

Why it works here: 10+ beers on the menu = 10+ videos built in before you even think about what's next. Every beer is a story. Cadence: 1-2x/week.
