01

What's In a Name

This IPA is named after the Dodd-Frank Act. Here's why. 30-second explainers. The mythology is already in the tap handles. Black Scholes, Louis Winthorpe, The Speculator. Nobody has told the audience what these names mean.

02

The Brewmaster

Wil Turner helped build Revolution Brewing into the largest independent brewery in Illinois. Before that: Goose Island. 30+ years. He's now brewing on Western Ave in Morgan Park. Show him brewing. Let the credibility speak.

03

The 1948 Ford

During the pandemic, instead of closing, they restored a 1948 Ford F5 and used it to deliver beer, haul Santa, run Easter parades. The Chicago Tribune covered it. The truck is still there. That's a cinematic object with a survival story attached.

04

Full Circle

Jim Beck worked the trading floor the brewery is named after. Now he owns it. That's not a coincidence, that's a narrative. The open-outcry method died when trading went digital. This brewery is the physical version of what the screens replaced.

05

The South Side's Local

The rooftop at golden hour. The wood-fired pizza. The dog-friendly patio. The community fundraisers. All filtered through the lens: this was built by people who chose hands over screens, for a neighborhood that deserves world-class craft.
