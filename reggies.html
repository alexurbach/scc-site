The Positioning Anchor

"The room where it happened first."

Every piece of content answers one question: who was in this room before you knew their name? The archive is sitting there. 19 years of shows, a record store on the second floor, three painted rock buses, a rooftop, a beach bar, a founder who has never been on camera. The feed is show flyers. The story is something else entirely.

What This Looks Like in Content

Five formats, one anchor.

Every format filters through the same lens: this is the discovery room. The place that heard it first. Here's what that becomes.

01

Before They Were ___

Logic sold out Reggies. So did Chance, Mac Miller, Khalid. Split-screen: the Reggies marquee then vs. the arena marquee now. Repeatable series. Every week, a new name from the archive.

02

The Empty Room

Brick walls. Cement floor. The garage bones still visible. Shoot it empty, mid-afternoon light. Caption energy: "This room has heard it first for 19 years." Let the architecture tell the story.

03

Record Breakers Upstairs

The record store that started booking bands in 1996 is still open on the second floor. Nobody knows this. That IS the content. Vinyl pulls, the listening station, the crates. 38 years of music retail hiding above a concert hall.

04

Robby

The man who looked at 60 buildings and picked a bumper shop. Who pushed vinyl aside to fit a band in a suburban record store. Who has been doing this for 38 years and has never been on camera. That's the episode.

05

The Full Complex

Two stages, a rooftop, a comedy shack, a record store, three painted rock buses, a beach bar. All of it started in a suburban record shop. Walk the building. Show the scale. Let people realize what they've been driving past.
